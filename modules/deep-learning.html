Course Lessons

01

Foundations of Deep Learning

Introduces deep learning as multi-layer neural networks that automatically learn features from raw data.

15 min Video

When we interact with modern technology—whether it is recognizing a face in photos, translating languages instantly, or recommending a movie—we are often seeing deep learning at work. Deep learning is a branch of machine learning that uses networks of layers, known as neural networks, to recognize patterns in data. Think of deep learning like teaching a student. At first, the student learns simple facts such as letters or numbers. Later, they combine this knowledge into sentences, and eventually, into complete essays. Similarly, in deep learning, the first layers of a network learn simple patterns, while deeper layers combine these into more complex ideas. Deep learning matters because it has dramatically changed what computers can do. It powers self-driving cars, medical diagnosis from images, voice assistants, and many other applications that once seemed impossible.

Key terms: deep neural network, hierarchy, representation learning
02

Deep Learning Workflow

Deep learning projects don’t succeed by just throwing data into a model — they follow a clear workflow.

8 min Notes

1. Data Preparation: Most of the effort goes here. Raw data is noisy, inconsistent, and incomplete. A well-prepared dataset often matters more than a fancy model. For example, in medical imaging, removing blurry scans and labeling them carefully is critical for accuracy. 2. Model Design: Choosing the right architecture is like choosing the right tool for the job. CNNs shine in vision, Transformers dominate in language, while hybrid models tackle multimodal tasks. 3. Training: This stage is about more than running code. It’s about setting learning rates, managing memory, and monitoring metrics in real time. Mistuned training can waste hours on GPUs without progress. 4. Evaluation: A model that works in the lab may fail in the real world. Proper evaluation uses test data, cross-validation, and fairness checks to ensure robust performance. 5. Deployment: Integration is often the hardest part. A model that works on a server may need to be compressed for a smartphone or edge device. This workflow isn’t rigid but iterative. Teams circle back when issues arise — data may need relabeling, or deployment might demand lighter architectures.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation
03

Convolutional Models for Vision

Convolutional models (CNNs) changed the way machines see the world. Unlike traditional methods that relied on handcrafted filters, CNNs learn features directly from data.

8 min Notes

The magic lies in their efficiency: instead of analyzing every pixel separately, CNNs slide filters across an image to detect edges, textures, and shapes. These small building blocks combine into recognition of complex objects like faces or traffic signs. Real-world impact is everywhere: • Healthcare: Detecting tumors from X-rays more reliably than some specialists. • Transportation: Enabling cars to detect pedestrians, lanes, and obstacles. • Security: Powering facial recognition in airports and smartphones. CNNs made image recognition scalable, turning computer vision from theory into daily reality.

Key terms: vanishing gradient, batch normalization, dropout, optimizer
04

Sequence and Language Models

Data doesn’t always come as static images — language, speech, and financial trends are sequential. Early sequence models like RNNs captured short-term patterns but struggled with long memory.

8 min Notes

Transformers solved this by introducing self-attention, allowing models to focus on relevant words or signals no matter how far apart they are. This leap turned machine translation, text generation, and even music composition into practical technologies. Examples include: • Google Translate: Context-aware translations in milliseconds. • ChatGPT & BERT: Understanding and generating human-like text. • Stock Predictions: Identifying hidden patterns across time. Sequence models show that deep learning isn’t just about recognizing images — it’s about capturing context over time.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation
05

Training and Optimization

Training deep models is where theory meets reality, and most failures happen here. It’s not enough to pick an algorithm — you need strategies to make learning efficient and reliable.

8 min Notes

• Optimization: Algorithms like SGD with momentum or Adam aren’t just math tricks — they determine whether your model converges in hours or weeks. • Regularization: Without methods like dropout, models memorize training data and fail in production. Imagine a student memorizing answers instead of learning concepts — that’s overfitting. • Normalization: Batch norm and layer norm keep values stable across layers, preventing the network from spiraling into instability. • Hardware Acceleration: GPUs and TPUs aren’t optional luxuries; they’re what make training billion-parameter models feasible. In practice, training is like piloting a plane: constant monitoring, mid-course corrections, and emergency responses when things go wrong. The right choices here separate toy models from production-grade AI.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation
06

Modern Deep Learning Architectures

Recent advances have introduced architectures that go beyond CNNs and RNNs. Transformers, for example, use a mechanism called self-attention to weigh the importance of different parts of the input simultaneously. This makes them powerful in handling large-scale language and vision tasks.

8 min Notes

You can imagine self-attention like reading a book while highlighting key phrases. Instead of reading line by line without context, you immediately notice which words or sentences carry more meaning. Transformers work in a similar way, capturing relationships across the entire input at once. These architectures are important because they drive today’s most advanced models, including those used for conversational AI, automated writing, and multimodal systems that combine text, images, and more. They represent the future direction of deep learning.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation
07

Generative Models

Generative models learn to create new data that resembles the training data. Instead of just classifying inputs, they generate outputs—like new images, text, or music.

8 min Notes

Two main types are GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks) and VAEs (Variational Autoencoders). GANs work like an artist and a critic: one creates, the other judges until the creations look real. Why it matters: Generative models power applications like AI art, realistic deepfakes, drug discovery, and data augmentation for machine learning.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation
08

Reinforcement Learning with Deep Networks

Reinforcement learning (RL) combines deep learning with decision-making. An agent learns by trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties for its actions.

20 min Video

Deep networks allow RL agents to handle complex environments, like playing Go or controlling robots. Think of teaching a dog tricks: each correct move gets a treat, guiding future behavior. Why it matters: Deep RL enables breakthroughs in gaming, robotics, recommendation systems, and adaptive control in real-world systems.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation

Deep Learning Mini Lab

Try to code on your own!

1.5 hour Code

- Start with Blobs + Linear → observe a single straight boundary.
- Switch to XOR/Moons → train an MLP with 1 hidden layer (8–12 units), try ReLU vs. tanh, and tune the learning rate (0.01–0.1) to see how nonlinear activations enable learning.
- On Rings, compare shallow vs. deeper hidden layers (e.g., 2–3 layers stacked). Notice how depth captures complex, nested boundaries.
- Add L2 regularization (λ≈0.001) to reduce overfitting when noise is high.
- Use the Train/Val split slider to observe generalization.
- Monitor Loss and Accuracy live in the dashboard as you tweak hyperparameters.
- For reproducibility: adjust random seeds, and use the Export/Import Weights buttons to save your trained model as JSON and load it later.

Key terms: DNN, activation, forward pass, backpropagation

Module Summary

Deep learning extends machine learning by using multi-layer neural networks that automatically learn representations from raw data. This module introduced the full workflow, from preparing datasets and designing models to training, evaluation, and deployment. You explored specialized architectures like CNNs for vision, RNNs and Transformers for sequences and language, and cutting-edge approaches in modern architectures and generative models. You also learned how optimization, regularization, and hardware acceleration make training effective, and how reinforcement learning with deep networks powers decision-making agents. By the end, you can explain how deep learning achieves breakthroughs in areas like computer vision, natural language processing, generative AI, robotics, and beyond, while recognizing the key practices needed to train, evaluate, and deploy models responsibly.
